HtH

Bringing Comfort to Public Spaces

How to Human is an exercise made with HTML and CSS.

It is a made-up online service of reviews by autistic people for autistic people, focusing on sensory needs and organizational challenges.

HtH is a visionary safe space consisting of a free website and low-touch, low-price mobile apps. HtH offers a reviewing system of businesses and public institutions, improving experiences for autistic and other neurodivergent people outside of their homes, and allowing users to share insights with a supportive community that is based on common needs and wishes.

It is also a place for businesses and institutions to understand how to accommodate different needs.

Dependency

HtH will not have a technological dependency since it's planned as a website + Android and iPhone applications. Because of that, it will also not be dependent on a vendor.

Value and market growth

There have been recent improvements in the ASD diagnostic criteria. Before then, mostly boys who showed specific traits would receive an autism diagnosis, leaving girls, adults, people of color, and queer people undiagnosed.

In 2006, statistical reports in the US estimated 1 in 110 children is autistic, and in 2021 it changed to 1 in 44.

There's also an increase in conversations on social media regarding autism, encouraging people who can't afford an official diagnosis, or people who don't fit the old criteria (which is still used by outdated practitioners and institutions) to research and self-diagnose.
A frustration has grown in the autism community from the prioritization of parents and caregivers' needs over the needs of autistic people in organizations and institutions claiming to focus on autism. Autistic people are building communities and accommodations for themselves, without having to accept autism conversations that do not involve autistic people.
The lack of resources and accommodations are what makes HtH a valuable service, being built by autistic people, for autistic people, focusing on the autistic experience with no external intervention.
The official and self-diagnosis growth makes HtH a service built for a growing market.
