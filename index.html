How to Human

Bringing Comfort to Public Spaces

How to Human is an exercise created with HTML and CSS.

It is a fictional online service of reviews by autistic people for autistic people, focusing on sensory needs and organizational challenges.

How to Human (HtH) is a visionary safe space that includes a free website and low-touch, low-price mobile apps. HtH provides a review system for businesses and public institutions, improving experiences for autistic and other neurodivergent people when they are not at home. It also allows users to share insights with a supportive community based on common needs and desires.

Additionally, it serves as a platform for businesses and institutions to understand how to accommodate diverse needs.

Value & Market Growth

There have been recent improvements in the ASD diagnostic criteria. Prior to these changes, mainly boys exhibiting specific traits would receive an autism diagnosis, leaving girls, adults, people of color, and queer people undiagnosed.

In 2006, statistical reports in the US estimated that 1 in 110 children is autistic. By 2021, this statistic had changed to 1 in 44.

Conversations about autism on social media have also increased. This encourages individuals who cannot afford an official diagnosis or do not fit the old criteria (which is still used by outdated practitioners and institutions) to conduct research for self-diagnose.

Within the autism community, frustration has grown due to the prioritization of the needs of parents and caregivers over those of autistic individuals in organizations and institutions that claim to focus on autism. Autistic individuals are establishing communities and accommodations for themselves, without needing to engage in autism discussions that do not include them.

The lack of resources and accommodations is what makes HtH a valuable service. It is being created by autistic people, for autistic people, with a focus on the autistic experience and without external intervention.

The growth in both official and self-diagnoses positions HtH as a service designed for a growing market.

Contacting users (Letter Simulation)
Hey Yael,

I'm Tal, and I work as a Product Manager at How to Human. I'm reaching out because we want to know more about how people use our product, and we'd love to hear about it from you.
Do you use HtH, and if so, are you autistic? (Self-diagnosis is valid!)
We'd be happy to offer you an opportunity to voice your frustrations, tell us what's working, and help us understand how we can better design HtH to suit people with your needs.
If you're not using HtH, feel free to forward it to someone who is!
What's involved? We'd like to visit you, see how you use HtH, and see how it accommodates you. We usually meet for about an hour and 15 minutes.
We'd like to observe sessions and see the way you use it. Why do it?
Because it's not a product made over the heads of autistic people by allistics for their parents, in HtH there are no conversations about us, without us.
This product is made by autistic people for autistic people, and this is what makes the experience of our autistic users so valuable.
With knowledge about your experience, we can understand what to change, add and fix, according to your needs. We also give snacks and merch as a thank you for your time.
Interested? Just reply back to this email, and I'll work with you directly on questions you might have, and get something set up on the calendar.
Looking forward to hearing from you!

Tal Karp-Rudin,
Product Manager

OMTM

The one metric that matters for HtH is app purchases.
Users can access the content from the website for free. Purchasing the app means value for the user.
Focusing on app purchases also gives insights on demand in different locations and demographics.
Having a paying user base will also legitimize efforts with business compliance and participation.
